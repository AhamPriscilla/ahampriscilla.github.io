Designing the Movie Ticketing App

The Problem

Due to the global pandemic and the ensuing lockdown, visits to public places were either completely prohibited or severely restricted in order to prevent the spread of the virus. However, as the management and prevention of the virus continue to improve, the strict limits on movement are being eased.
This is especially exciting for fun-loving people who have felt trapped since the start of the lockdown, as they anticipate possibilities to resume their social activities and restore as much normalcy to their lives as possible.
While these customers are ready to resume their lives, businesses that cater to large crowds still have a lot of work to do in terms of managing their post-lockdown operations to ensure it adheres to pandemic guidelines, protects the health of their customers, employees, and does not impede their profits.
This presents a challenge for movie cinemas, that still rely significantly on in-person movie ticketing. This service style readily converts to long lines, a significant health risk to workers and customers, and a potential source of poor customer experience.

Goals & Objectives

For the customers

  • To improve customer satisfaction by giving them more control over their movie viewing experience at the cinema.

For the Brand

  • To increase & manage customer engagement
  • To streamline the movie ticketing process
  • To aid with crowd control and help in their efforts to curb the spread of the virus

My Role & Responsibilities

I handled this project as the Ux, Interaction, and UI Designer.  I assumed full responsiblity for all user research and unmoderated usability testing, for which there were 14 participants. 
With the guidiance of a mentor and feedback from the participants, I was able to realize this project.
It took a total of 10 weeks to complete.

Understanding the User

I began the process by asking questions. I conducted research to determine who the customers are and the available alternatives they utilized to purchase their movie tickets. 
Following my finding from secondary research, customer feedbacks, and personal observations from my visits to the cinema, the demography for moviegoers is largely of young adults; mostly females, who go to the movies (particularly in groups) for the social experience, as a means to relax, network, or make a social statement.

Finding Answers

To understand app user experience with current alternatives customers relied on, I visited the websites of major cinemas in the city and downloaded the only available movie ticketing app at the moment.
Getting to experience the alternatives allowed me understand some of the pain points experienced by customers as they tried to book thier tickets using these available methods. With the help of the "How might we approach", I was able to find ideas that allowed me how ideas could be alligned with goals.
The major pain point users experienced as deduced from feedbacks on Playstore were:

  • "The app is only setup for club memeberships....just pay for your ticket when you visit the cinema in-person."

    • Considering this, how might we... "create an remote experience for users that alows them reach their goals easily?"

  • "It is confusing to move around... been trying to get ticket for 2 days."

    • Considering this, how might we... "enable people to use their mobile devices to obtain the most crucial information?

User Personas

Paper, Wireframes & Mockups

Taking the user pain points as a guide I decided what actions and features would be crucial and beneficial to achieving their goal. I opted to design a sitemap around these conclusions.
I devised an eight-step method to ticket purchasing with the aim of achieving an experience that is both focused and simple. The design drafts focuses on enabling the target customers achieve their goals of booking and paying for their tickets in no more than eight steps - from the home screen to the ticket confirmation page.

Wireframe, Early Designs and Prototype

Usability Tests

With the wireframes and its prototypes, I was able to conduct the first unmoderated usablity test through maze app. With a total of 14 participants and 12 successful responses, I recieved valuable feedback that served as pointers into what the target customers would love to have.
The test focused on 3 major aspects:

  • How easy is it for users to navigate the app
  • How easy is it for users to achieve their goal on the app
  • Overall experience for the user

The first usability test was successful as it revealed several points for improvements as well as a need to pay more attention to potential accessibility needs of diverse users.

User responses from the first usability test: Wireframe & Prototype

On Accessibility

  • 1 out of 14 participants is color blind.

    • This meant that cues for call to action couldn't focus on the use of colors alone.

  • 3 out of 14 participants needs glasses to see properly

    • This emphasizes a greater need for distinct contrast between UI elements and background, clear call to action cues for easy readabilty for all users especially the visually impaired ones.

These finding, coupled with the feedbacks obtained for the usablity testing led the the iterations I made on the Prototype.
